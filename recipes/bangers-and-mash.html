Bangers and Mash

Description

Bangers and mash is a traditional British dish served with sausages and mashed potatoes topped with onion gravy. The term banger originated from food rationing during World War 1 when sausages were produced with high water content and prone to explode when heated. Bangers and mash remains a staple of British cuisine as a comfort food that can be enjoyed at the pub or in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized onions
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon of sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 1/3 cup of water
  • 1/3 cup of milk
  • 5 beef sausages
  • 1 1/2 cups of mashed potatoes mix

Steps

  1. Set the stove to medium-high heat. Fry the sausages for between 4-5 minutes and then set them off to the side. Save the leftover sausage grease in the pan for later.
  2. Procure two medium-sized onions for chopping and slicing.
  3. Adjust the stove to low heat. Add the onions onto the pan with the sausage grease and continuously stir the pan until there is a gentle sizzle. Add 1 1/2 teaspoon of sugar and leave the pan off to the side once the onions have been cooked.
  4. Put 2 tablespoons of butter, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1 1/3 cup of water into a separate stove. Boil the water. Add 1/3 cup of milk and pour in 1 1/2 cups of mashed potatoes mix. Stir gently with a fork until the mashed potatoes mix are soft and moist.
  5. Add a teaspoon of butter onto the onions. Pour in the gravy and stir for around 3 minutes until the water boils. Add additional seasoning if needed.
  6. Put sausages into the gravy to finish cooking. Let the sausages simmeri n low heat for around 3 minutes.
  7. Plate the sausages on top of the mashed potatoes and pour the gravy over the sausages and mashed potatoes.
