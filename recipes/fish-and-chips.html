Fish and Chips

Description

Fish and chips is the quintessential British dish served with fried battered fish and deep-fried potatoes. Fried battered fish originated from Spanish and Portuguese Jews who prepared fish which was coated with flour and fried in oil as a meal for days of religious observance on which cooking was prohibited. The concept of serving fried fish served with chips started with the opening of the first fish and chip shop in London's East End and the popularity of the dish spread across the United Kingdom. Today, the dish is often regarded as the national dish of the United Kingdom.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 quart of vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 pounds of cod fillets

Steps

  1. Peel and slice 4 large potatoes into strips. Place sliced potato strips in a bowl with cold water.
  2. Add 1 cup of flour, 1 teaspoon of baking powder, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper into a separate bowl and mix them together.
  3. Add 1 cup of milk and an egg. Stir until the batter is smooth and let the batter rest for 20 minutes.
  4. Add 1 quart of vegetable oil to a pan and heat the pan to 350 degrees Farenheit (175 degrees Celsius).
  5. Drain and dry the potatoes before frying the potatoes in the oil for around 5 minutes until the potatoes are tender. Place the potatoes on paper towels to dry the excess oil.
  6. Soak the fillets in the batter before frying them one piece at a time.
  7. Fry the fillets in batches until the sides of the fillets are golden brown. Place the fried fillets on paper towls to dry the excess oil.
  8. Fry the potatoes again for around 1-2 minutes until the potatoes are crisp. Place the chips on paper towels to dry the excess oil.
  9. Plate the fillets with the chips on the side.
